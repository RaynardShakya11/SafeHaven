# SafeHaven

SafeHaven is a web-based platform developed to bridge the critical gap between displaced individuals in emergency situations and organisations or individuals that can provide temporary housing. In today's world, crises — ranging from natural disasters such as earthquakes and floods to social emergencies such as domestic violence and displacement due to conflict — generate an urgent and unpredictable demand for temporary shelter. Existing systems for managing such demands are predominantly manual, paper-based, or rely on fragmented digital tools that lack real-time coordination between stakeholders.

The purpose of SafeHaven is to create a centralised, efficient, and ethically designed digital platform that enables Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOs) and shelter administrators to manage available housing resources transparently, while simultaneously allowing verified shelter providers to update their capacity in real time. The system ensures that data is handled with a strong emphasis on privacy and security, incorporating robust authentication, role-based access control, and encrypted credentials, to uphold the dignity and safety of individuals accessing the system.
